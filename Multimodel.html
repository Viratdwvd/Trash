import firebase_admin

import time

    else:
        print("Failed to retrieve data from sensor")

Great question, Priyanshu! Let’s break it down simply 👇

🔌 Why Use a 10kΩ Resistor Between VCC and DATA on DHT11?

The 10kΩ resistor is called a pull-up resistor. It's required by the DHT11/DHT22 sensor to ensure stable data transmission on the DATA line.

💡 What Does It Mean to Place the Resistor Between VCC and DATA?

It means you need to connect one end of the 10kΩ resistor to the VCC pin (Power) and the other end to the DATA pin (Signal) like this:

🧩 Pin Mapping of DHT11:

sql
DHT11 pinout (front view):

1  --- VCC     (connect to 3.3V or 5V on Pi)
2  --- DATA    (connect to GPIO on Pi, e.g., GPIO4)
3  --- NC      (Not connected)
4  --- GND     (connect to GND on Pi)

🛠 Wiring With Pull-Up Resistor:

  • Connect pin 1 (VCC) to 3.3V or 5V on Raspberry Pi.

  • Connect pin 2 (DATA) to GPIO4 (or any GPIO pin).

  • Place the 10kΩ resistor between pin 1 and pin 2 on your breadboard.
    (This means: insert one leg of the resistor into the same row as VCC, and the other into the same row as DATA).

This tells the sensor:

"Hey! If the DATA line isn’t being actively pulled low, assume it's HIGH by default."

🔧 Visual Representation:

lua
        +------------------------+
        |                        |
     VCC o------.                |
                |                |
               [10kΩ]            |
                |                |   DHT11
     DATA o------'---------------o   Sensor
                |                |
     GND o-----------------------o

  • The [10kΩ] connects between VCC and DATA.

  • This stabilizes the signal to avoid false readings.
